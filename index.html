No source is perfectly neutral, and this brief does not claim to be. It earns trust through three structural mechanisms the reader can verify for themselves.

1
Pillar 1 — The Facts
What happened, in neutral and precise language. Every claim sourced only from official government databases, wire services, or verified regional reporting. No loaded language. No interpretation. Specific dollar figures, headcounts, and jurisdictions named wherever they exist.
Primary sources: Seattle Open Data, Washington State DOR, ESD, OFM, King County, Sound Transit, PSRC, BLS, and peer-reviewed research.
2
Pillar 2 — Perspectives in Tension
Every significant perspective rendered as its most thoughtful proponents would state it. The Seattle civic landscape leans progressive, so this brief actively seeks out the strongest pro-growth, business-oriented, and fiscal-conservative arguments. Every source is named and labeled. Minimum two sources per side. Symmetric sourcing is structural, not editorial.
Local sources: Seattle Times editorial, Puget Sound Business Journal, Washington Policy Center, The Stranger, Publicola, South Seattle Emerald, Crosscut, and others named openly.
3
Pillar 3 — Fault Lines and Common Ground
Names the actual divide honestly. Not "this is a complex issue" but which specific value, assumption, or institutional interest is in tension with which other. Then identifies what people across the divide actually share. If genuine common ground does not exist, the brief says so and explains why the divide is structural.

Government data first. For every story with a fiscal, employment, or real estate dimension, the brief searches the primary government database before relying on secondary reporting. Washington State DOR, Seattle budget quarterlies, King County financials, ESD employment data, and Sound Transit project trackers are checked directly.

Brokerage corroboration required. Commercial real estate statistics require confirmation from two independent brokerage reports before reaching Tier 1. A single brokerage figure is always flagged as single-source.

Epistemic tier flags. Every factual claim is either confirmed by two or more independent sources (no flag), single-source (flagged inline), or drawn from an official statement not yet independently verified (flagged inline). Silence means confirmed.

★
This Week's Top Story
The most consequential regional story of the week, selected by scoring every qualifying story across six criteria. Delivered first, every issue. Its home category still gets its own separate entry.
1
Fiscal Policy and City Budget
Revenue structures, budget decisions, tax measures, the payroll expense tax, Business and Occupation tax, budget gaps and one-time fixes, and the fiscal choices shaping the region's capacity to fund public services.
2
Economy, Business and Competitiveness
Technology sector dynamics, major employer decisions, office market conditions, corporate relocations, port and trade activity, labor market data, and the forces determining whether the region grows or loses ground.
3
Housing Policy and Development
Zoning and density decisions, permitting policy, housing affordability, development economics, homelessness policy and encampment management, and the policy choices shaping who can afford to live in the region.
4
Public Safety and Criminal Justice
Crime data and trends from official SPD and King County sources, police staffing and policy, drug policy, prosecutorial decisions, court capacity. Data reported neutrally from primary sources without presupposing a directional conclusion.
5
Transportation Policy and Infrastructure
Sound Transit and the ST3 buildout, Washington State Ferries, WSDOT major projects, King County Metro. The fiscal and governance dimensions of transportation investment: who pays, who decides, and who bears the cost of delay.
6
Social Policy and Human Services
Homelessness policy, shelter capacity, drug treatment access, mental health service delivery, Medicaid funding, and the design and measurable outcomes of publicly funded social programs.
7
Education and Workforce Development
Seattle Public Schools budget and governance, University of Washington research and workforce pipeline, community college access, AI-literate workforce development, and childcare affordability as a workforce constraint.
8
Politics and Government
Seattle City Council votes and governance, Mayor's Office actions, King County Executive and Council decisions, ballot measure campaigns, and the machinery of local democratic governance. Weighted toward Olympia during session.
9
State and Regional Policy
Washington State Legislature decisions with direct regional impact, Governor's Office actions, Puget Sound Regional Council governance, and federal policy changes with significant regional fiscal or operational consequences.
10
Portraits and Passings
One portrait of a person in or connected to the Puget Sound region whose decisions or civic actions had measurable impact on the week's policy news. At least one passing if a significant regional figure died this week.
Primary data sources Seattle Open Data WA State DOR ESD OFM Sound Transit PSRC BLS
